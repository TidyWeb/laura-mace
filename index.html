Her Story

Where This Comes From

Laura Mace didn't find this work through a textbook. She found it through Birmingham itself — through the schools, the community centres, the families quietly carrying wounds that the system rarely reaches.

As a qualified, trauma-informed integrative counsellor, Laura bridges the gap between formal therapeutic services and the raw reality of community life. She works with individuals, couples, young people and families — and she does it with the credibility of someone who has sat alongside survivors of knife crime and violence through her work at the Rape and Sexual Violence Project (RSVP).

ALIVE Wellness Centre CIC is the organisation she built to make that work permanent, scalable, and community-owned. It is not a pilot programme. It is already working.

What Laura Does

A community interest company supporting survivors of knife crime and youth violence across Birmingham — through therapy, physical activity, and education.

01

Counselling Services

Trauma-informed therapy for individuals, couples, young people and families. Person-centred and psychodynamic approaches. Specialist experience supporting survivors of violence and abuse.

02

Physical & Community Activities

Personal training, youth clubs, and community centres. Partnership with Krav4Kids — building confidence and resilience through basic self-defence for young people. Because healing isn't only talk.

03

Training & Workshops

Trauma-Informed Practice for Professionals. Understanding and Responding to Youth Violence. Safeguarding and Mental Health Awareness — delivered to schools and front-line services across the region.

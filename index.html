Her Story

Where This Comes From

Laura Mace didn't find this work through a textbook. She found it through Birmingham itself — through the schools, the community centres, the families quietly carrying wounds that the system rarely reaches.

As a qualified, trauma-informed integrative counsellor, Laura bridges the gap between formal therapeutic services and the raw reality of community life. She works with individuals, couples, young people and families — and she does it with the credibility of someone who has sat alongside survivors of knife crime and violence through her work at the Rape and Sexual Violence Project (RSVP).

ALIVE Wellness Centre CIC is the organisation she built to make that work permanent, scalable, and community-owned. It is not a pilot programme. It is already working.

What Laura Does

Founded by Laura Mace following her own son’s experience with knife crime, ALIVE was born from a mother’s mission to turn personal trauma into community-wide healing. We are a community interest company supporting survivors of youth violence across Birmingham by merging professional therapy with physical activity and education. Our work bridges the critical gap in victim support, ensuring that tragedy becomes a catalyst for resilience and lasting change.

01

Counselling Services

Trauma-informed therapy for individuals, couples, young people and families. Person-centred and psychodynamic approaches. Specialist experience supporting survivors of violence and abuse.

02

Physical & Community Activities

Personal training, youth clubs, and community centres. Partnership with Krav4Kids — building confidence and resilience through basic self-defence for young people. Because healing isn't only talk.

03

Training & Workshops

Trauma-Informed Practice for Professionals. Understanding and Responding to Youth Violence. Safeguarding and Mental Health Awareness — delivered to schools and front-line services across the region.

Strategic Alliances & Partnerships

04

Survival into Strength: Kieran Wood

As a vital extension of our trauma-informed work, we partner with Kieran Wood—featured in our BBC media coverage—to deliver self-defence sessions for 11-16 year olds. Held Saturdays (11am-1pm) at Hatchford Brook Community Hub, these sessions turn survival narratives into practical physical resilience and confidence.

05

Winning Through Wellness: Winnr Sports

In collaboration with Winnr Sports, we merge professional boxing and fitness with proactive mental health support. This partnership focuses on discipline and integrity, utilizing sport to inspire impact and provide young people with a clear pathway toward success.

06

Championing Growth: Leon Newman

British Powerlifting Champion Leon Newman brings elite strength and leadership to our Birmingham youth clubs. By providing world-class coaching at the grassroots level, Leon mentors our participants in the power of perseverance and championship-level dedication.

The Transition — From Roaming to Rooted

ALIVE currently operates as a high-impact mobile force, delivering vital services from a home office into schools, youth clubs, and community centres. However, to move from temporary intervention to permanent transformation, we need a sanctuary. A dedicated hub will allow us to scale our reach and provide the consistent, safe environment that Birmingham’s families and survivors of violence deserve.

Click plan to view full 2024 Technical Proposal (PDF)

The Vision — Eagle Court as a Healing Ecosystem

We have identified Eagle Court in Birmingham as the ideal catalyst for this change. This multi-floor facility will be transformed into a holistic ecosystem: a creative basement for music and arts rehearsal; a ground-floor gymnasium and sports hall for 5-a-side football, netball, and basketball; and upper-level professional therapy suites designed for quiet recovery, collective learning, and communal support.

The Expansion — Empowering the West Midlands

A permanent home unlocks critical new capabilities. We are ready to expand our reach into specialist domestic violence counselling and adult education services. By collaborating with the West Midlands Combined Authority, the DWP, and the Department for Education, we will bridge the gap between mental health recovery and economic independence, providing the skills and job-seeking support necessary to break the cycle of trauma.

The Appeal — Your Voice is Our Key

We are not asking for financial donations. We are seeking the vocal, public backing of Birmingham’s influential leaders and businesses. Your endorsement of our bid through the AVIRE property scheme is the most powerful tool we have to secure Eagle Court. By standing with ALIVE, you are helping us turn an empty office building into a heartbeat for the community.
